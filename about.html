About

We currently have 12 staff members teaching beginner, intermediate, and advanced courses at 3 separate locations.

What We Do

Coding Cadet works to spread computer science education to a wide range of students through its web development classes. We teach languages like HTML, CSS, and Javascript along with modern web development resource libraries like Bootstrap, Font Awesome, and jQuery to empower students with the ability to create advanced websites and web applications.

By the end of our first beginning course, students will be able to create responsive static websites following common web design principles and standards. The class culminates with a final project in which students create websites for a fictional bakery. By the end of our intermediate course, students will be able to create interactive web applications. Through the lessons and the demo to-do application, students gain working knowledge of JavaScript applications in web design. In the advanced course, students partner with a business client to build their website. With minimal guidance from instructors, students have completed full webpages for local small businesses.

We now teach at Cypress and Buena Park Libraries and will begin classes in La Palma libary on October 31. We take advantage of public library resources to reach more students and offer free classes. To find more details about locations, visit the contact page.
